luci-app-xray brings the full power of [Xray-core](https://github.com/XTLS/Xray-core) to your OpenWrt router through a clean LuCI web interface. It sets up transparent proxying for your entire LAN — routing traffic through your chosen proxy servers with fine-grained control over which devices bypass, use, or forward through the proxy.

## What luci-app-xray does

luci-app-xray generates a complete Xray JSON configuration from your UCI settings and manages the Xray process via OpenWrt's procd init system. It configures nftables transparent proxy rules so that LAN traffic is intercepted and routed through Xray without any per-device configuration.

## Getting started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install luci-app-xray on your router">
    Add the feed to your OpenWrt build or use the pre-built `.ipk` packages from GitHub Releases. Requires OpenWrt 22.03 or later with firewall4.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a proxy server">
    In the LuCI interface, go to **Services → Xray** and add a server entry with your proxy protocol, address, port, and credentials.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable transparent proxy">
    Under **General Settings**, enable **Transparent Proxy** and select the LAN interfaces to intercept (e.g., `br-lan`). Set your balancer to point to the server you added.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reboot and verify">
    Reboot your router. Traffic from LAN devices will now be routed through Xray. Check the **Status** page to confirm Xray is running and inspect active connections.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  luci-app-xray requires OpenWrt 22.03 or later with firewall4 and nftables. It does **not** support LEDE, OpenWrt prior to 22.03, or Lean's OpenWrt fork. If you need support for older versions, consider Passwall or similar alternatives.
</Note>
